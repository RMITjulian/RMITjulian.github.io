Info & Interests

How's it going, my name is Julian Oddo and I go to RMIT university, doing a bachelor's in Psychology as an Applied Science. My student number is s3720038 and my school email is s3720038@student.rmit.edu.au.

I was born in Australia (Melbourne) and have been raised around the North / North Eastern suburbs my entire life. I live in Northcote right now and completed my VCE schooling at Diamond Valley College. I know survival sign language (survival is stretching it) and currently attempting to speak Italian and Japanese due to the fact half my family can speak Italian and I plan on going to Japan for a while in the future (next couple years). A cool fact about me is I can play piano but can't read music to save my life. My music teacher in high school loved and hated me due to this fact. I just was naturally drawn to playing piano after learning my first song and would often spend my 'spare' sessions during school in the music room playing for fun. I'm not sure on whether I'll ever try learn to read music but I'll definitely continue playing.

My interest in IT would most likely stem back to just using the computer to play games on the website miniclip.com. My cousin showed this to me when I was very young and through that I discovered an interest in technology in general. In a way I guess I was like any other kid at the time who was discovering technology through games / other things like phones and basically never stopped using tech since. I've had some experience with many fields of IT ranging from completing a Cert III course in multimedia and VCE Information Technology in which I learnt many thing such as web design, coding with C++, animation, databases and many more. I also held a casual job for a game development company (which I was accepted due to my portfolio from my multimedia class) throughout my last year of highschool which taught me many things about the industry. I worked for 7-YM's mobile game company 'Rusty Rings' for some time and then moved to the VR team 'Ultimerse' in which I helped develop 'Paperville Panic', a VR game that can be bought an played on steam . I chose RMIT because I felt like it was the best university for me in terms of accessibility and, for lack of a better word, the general 'feel'. I can't really explain my reasoning beyond that, it's just for some reason RMIT stuck out to me as a good learning experience for some reason. Through this course I'm hoping to learn skills to better enhance my skillset for employability as being able to use technology is becoming a requirement for nearly any job out there. Profile

I think that these tests acurately reflect my behaviour and the way I prefer to study / work. I would not put too much meaning on these tests however, and don't think they can be used to predict and show how someone will act in any situation. Instead they should be used as a general indicator of what type behaviour you would generally see expressed from them. Results of a personality test, whether speaking of mine or my team members, would not really change the way I act in a team. Peoples effort and agreeablness (two very important traits for team work) would in my opinion change on how enthusiastic they are in a project. I would give my peers a 'level playing field' and judge how I go foward after seeing how they work. In terms of picking a team, although I just said how little a personality test will influence my first impression / decision, I guess I would be less likely to pair with someone with a larger assertive score than me due to mine being above average already.
